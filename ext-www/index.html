Documenting Scientific Workflows

The Metadata, Provenance, and Ontology Project

About Metadata Provenance Ontology Scientific activities always involve a series of workflows which create and transform data. Careful documentation of those workflows and the data, recording its origins, owners, and associated metadata is a critical part of any scientific enterprise. Historically this was the role of the lab notebook, but the digital era has resulted instead in the fragmentation of data, processing, and annotation. As the size and variety of data grow, keeping track of data and its relations to workflow become even more challenging.

The MPO Project (Automated Metadata, Provenance Catalog, And Navigable Interfaces: Ensuring The Usefulness Of Extreme-Scale Data) is an ongoing multi-institutional effort aimed at solving this problem by developing tools to automatically document scientific workflows and all associated information. Data and all processes that create or modify that data are represented as a directed acyclic graph and are captured from instrumented workflow scripts. The export of data, for publication, presentation or external databases are recorded, allowing traceability in either direction – answering the questions “Where was this data used?” or “Where did the data in this figure come from.” Namespace management is provided through a well-structured “ontology,” which can be customized for any particular community or application.

MPO Team The MPO Project and its associated tools are being created by a multi-institutional team:

  • General Atomics/DIII-D National Fusion Facility
  • Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory
  • Massachusetts Institute of Technology/Plasma Science and Fusion Center

Users & Institutions The initial users of the MPO system come from within the magnetic fusion energy scientific community and include Alcator C-Mod, the DIII-D National Fusion Facility, the SWIM Integrated Plasma Simulator (IPS), and the Advanced Tokamak Modeling Project (AToM). The MPO Project is currently working with the CASCADE project, funded by DOE’s Regional/Global Climate Modeling Program, to instrument their workflows that are modeling extreme weather events. For more details, see the list of current MPO production systems.


Try MPO To make the concepts and the tools associated with the MPO more concrete, a demonstration area has been constructed that does not require any authentication or authorization. It is possible to navigate real workflows and their associated metadata but the ability to add information or add comments is not included. We encourage you to explore the demonstration area

Become a MPO User If the MPO tools and associated ability to document your workflow or explore workflows of your colleagues is appealing, we encourage you to register to become an MPO user - MPO allows access based on certificates that have been registered with the MPO project. If you have any questions, please contact the MPO Team at mpo@fusion.gat.com
